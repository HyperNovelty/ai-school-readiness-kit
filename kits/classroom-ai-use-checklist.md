# Classroom AI Use Checklist

Use this checklist before an AI tool is introduced into a classroom activity.

- The teacher can explain the AI use in one plain sentence.
- Students know when AI is being used.
- Students know what work remains their own responsibility.
- The tool is not used for hidden grading, placement, discipline, or surveillance.
- Student data is not entered unless there is an approved local process.
- A non-AI path is available when access, disability, language, or device limits create barriers.
- A human reviews AI-supported output before it affects students.
- Families receive communication when the use could affect trust, privacy, or expectations.
- The use has a clear stop point if it creates confusion or unfairness.

Recommended readiness label: `ready_for_local_review` only after every item is addressed.
