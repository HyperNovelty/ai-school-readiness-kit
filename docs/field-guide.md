# Field Guide

Use this kit before an AI tool affects student work, feedback, grading, placement, family communication, or trust.

The basic question is not whether AI is good or bad. The question is whether a specific use is clear enough, bounded enough, and reviewed enough for the local context.

## Review Order

1. Name the school context.
2. Name the audience affected by the AI use.
3. Describe the AI use case in plain language.
4. Decide whether student data is involved.
5. Check whether assessment, grading, feedback, or placement could be affected.
6. Identify equity and access concerns.
7. Write human review steps.
8. Decide whether family communication is needed.
9. List what the use is not for.
10. Assign a review status.

## Local Review Status

- `draft`: early thinking only.
- `ready_for_local_review`: ready for local discussion using the checklist.
- `needs_policy_review`: may touch rules, contracts, privacy, assessment, or governance.
- `do_not_use`: should not be used in the described context.

Keep records synthetic unless your local organization has an approved private process.
