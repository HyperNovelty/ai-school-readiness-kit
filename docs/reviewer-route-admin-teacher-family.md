# Reviewer Route For Administrators, Teachers, And Families

Use this one-page route to hold a public-safe discussion before a proposed AI use touches students, assignments, assessment, privacy, access, or trust. Do not include student names, grades, disability records, discipline records, health information, family contact details, private incidents, account data, or vendor credentials.

This route is not legal, compliance, medical, mental-health, procurement, or policy authorization advice. It helps reviewers prepare questions for the appropriate local process.

## Administrator Route

- What local policy, procurement, data, accessibility, and recordkeeping processes must review this AI use before it reaches students?
- Could this use affect grading, placement, discipline, counseling, surveillance, special services, or family communication?
- Who owns the decision to approve, pause, redesign, or reject the use?
- What evidence would show that teachers, students, and families understand the boundary of the AI use?
- What is the stop condition if the tool produces inaccurate, biased, confusing, or privacy-risking output?

## Teacher Route

- What learning goal is the AI use meant to support?
- What will students see, submit, or receive from the system?
- What human review happens before AI-assisted material affects feedback, grades, recommendations, or family communication?
- How will students know when AI is being used and what they are still responsible for learning?
- What non-AI alternative is available if the tool is unavailable, inaccessible, or inappropriate for a learner?

## Parent And Family Route

- What is the plain-language purpose of the AI use?
- What student information, if any, is involved, and how can the school explain that without exposing private records?
- Will AI influence grades, placement, discipline, counseling, services, or communication with families?
- Who can families contact with concerns, opt-out questions, accessibility needs, or correction requests?
- What will the school do if the AI output is wrong, unfair, confusing, or used outside the stated purpose?

## Shared Decision Prompt

After each route is reviewed, choose one local posture:

- `ready_for_limited_review`: public-safe, low-impact discussion can continue with named human review.
- `needs_policy_review`: local policy, privacy, procurement, accessibility, or leadership review is needed before use.
- `redesign_required`: the proposal needs a narrower purpose, less data, clearer human review, or a non-AI alternative.
- `do_not_use`: the proposal is not appropriate for this kit or should not proceed in the described form.
